Marie Curie

Marie Curie was a Polish-born physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

She played a key role in the development of modern physics and chemistry. Her discoveries contributed significantly to medical research and cancer treatment.

Timeline of Marie Curie's Life

  • 1867 – Born in Warsaw, Poland
  • 1891 – Moved to Paris to study at the Sorbonne
  • 1903 – Won the Nobel Prize in Physics
  • 1911 – Won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry
  • 1934 – Died in Passy, France
